About Me

Hey, I'm Leonardo —
engineering has been the plan since age 5

Bologna-born 🇮🇹, French-educated, and obsessed with flying machines
long before I could spell "aerodynamics."

I grew up in Ferney-Voltaire, France — yes, the tiny town right next to CERN — where I graduated with a scientific baccalauréat scoring 19.27/20 in 2018. That same year I packed my bags and headed to EPFL in Lausanne for Mechanical Engineering: five intense years covering maths, physics, structural mechanics, fluid dynamics, electronics, manufacturing, and more. The breadth was intentional — I wanted a general mechanical engineering foundation, not a narrow specialisation too early.

By my Master's I'd zeroed in on what excited me most: aerodynamics and space. I added a Fluid Mechanics specialisation and a Space Technologies minor, then crowned five years at EPFL with a thesis project at the LIS lab — automating the wind-tunnel characterisation of a morphing drone, where hardware, control code, and aerodynamic data all collide.

That wasn't enough. In November 2023 I joined ISAE-Supaero (Toulouse) as a PhD candidate, diving into the dynamics of flexible aircraft — think Solar Impulse or NASA's Helios, where wings flex so dramatically that classical rigid-body models simply break down. PhD highlights so far: authoring the ASWING Extended User Manual (live on the official MIT site), creating WingLoop — an open-source automation & control library presented at AIAA AVIATION FORUM 2025, Las Vegas — and ongoing work improving stall modelling for high-aspect-ratio wings.

Because simulations alone never fully scratch the itch, I've also been designing and building my own quadcopter from scratch — theory, electronics, firmware, and first flights.

Beyond the lab

Life outside engineering keeps me grounded (literally and figuratively). You'll find me running, cycling, or dancing to keep the work-life balance alive, and strumming classical or electric guitar when the brain needs a rest. On the community side, I co-lead the International Group at the Toulouse Student Parish, do homeless outreach with the Ordre de Malte, and represent PhD students as elected representative within the MEGeP doctoral school (2024–2026).
