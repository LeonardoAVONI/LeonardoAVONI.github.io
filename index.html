About Me

Hey, I'm Leonardo —
engineering has been the plan since age 5

Bologna-born 🇮🇹, French-educated, and obsessed with flying machines
long before I could spell "aerodynamics."

I grew up in Ferney-Voltaire, France — right next to CERN — where I graduated with a scientific baccalauréat scoring 19.27/20 in 2018. That same year I started Mechanical Engineering at EPFL: five years spanning maths, structural mechanics, fluid dynamics, electronics, manufacturing and more. The breadth was intentional — a general foundation before specialising.

By my Master's I had zeroed in on aerodynamics and space. I added a Fluid Mechanics specialisation and a Space Technologies minor, then finished with a thesis at the LIS lab automating the wind-tunnel characterisation of a morphing drone.

That wasn't enough. In November 2023 I joined ISAE-Supaero (Toulouse) as a PhD candidate researching the dynamics of flexible aircraft — think Solar Impulse or NASA's Helios. PhD highlights: the ASWING Extended User Manual (live on MIT's site), WingLoop (presented at AIAA AVIATION FORUM 2025, Las Vegas), and ongoing stall-modelling work for high-aspect-ratio wings.

Because simulations alone never fully scratch the itch, I also designed and built my own quadcopter from scratch — theory, electronics, firmware, and first flights.

Beyond the lab

I stay sane through running, cycling, and dancing, and unwind with classical or electric guitar. On the community side: co-leader of the International Group at the Toulouse Student Parish, homeless outreach with the Ordre de Malte, and elected PhD representative within the MEGeP doctoral school (2024–2026).
