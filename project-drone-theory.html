Personal Project · 2023–2024

First Drone Development
Theory

From Newton's laws to motor equations — building the mathematical foundations of a quadcopter from scratch.

Overview

Before touching a single component, I committed to fully understanding the physics behind a quadcopter. This project documents the theoretical groundwork: rigid-body dynamics, rotor aerodynamics, motor-propeller matching, and linear control design.

Key Topics Covered

1. Rigid-Body Dynamics

Derivation of the 6-DOF equations of motion for a quadrotor using Newton-Euler formalism. The translational and rotational dynamics are coupled through the inertia tensor and gyroscopic effects of spinning rotors. Understanding these equations is the prerequisite for any meaningful control design.

2. Rotor Aerodynamics

Application of Blade Element Momentum Theory (BEMT) to size propellers for a target thrust-to-weight ratio. Key parameters: blade pitch, chord distribution, tip speed, and the trade-off between efficiency and responsiveness.

3. Motor & ESC Selection

Matching brushless DC motors (BLDC) to the propeller load curve using KV ratings, torque constants, and efficiency maps. ESC selection criteria based on current draw at hover and full-throttle burst.

4. Control Architecture

Design of a cascaded PID controller: outer loop for position/altitude, inner loop for attitude (roll, pitch, yaw). Linearisation of the nonlinear dynamics around hover to enable frequency-domain analysis and gain tuning.

5. Battery & Power Budget

LiPo cell selection based on C-rating, capacity, and target flight time. Power consumption breakdown across motors, flight controller, and peripherals.

Tools Used

Python (NumPy, SciPy, Matplotlib) · MATLAB/Simulink · Pencil & paper (the best simulation tool)

Outcome

A fully documented design specification document — frame size, motor KV, propeller pitch/diameter, battery configuration, estimated hover time — that fed directly into the practical build phase.
