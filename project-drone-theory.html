Personal Project · 2023–2024

First Drone Development
Theory

From Newton's laws to motor equations — building the mathematical foundations of a quadcopter from scratch.

Overview

Before touching a single component, I committed to fully understanding the physics behind a quadcopter. This project documents the theoretical groundwork: rigid-body dynamics, rotor aerodynamics, motor-propeller matching, and linear control design.

Key Topics

1 · Rigid-Body Dynamics

Derivation of the 6-DOF equations of motion for a quadrotor using Newton-Euler formalism. Translational and rotational dynamics are coupled through the inertia tensor and gyroscopic effects of spinning rotors.

2 · Rotor Aerodynamics

Application of Blade Element Momentum Theory (BEMT) to size propellers for a target thrust-to-weight ratio. Key parameters: blade pitch, chord distribution, tip speed, and the efficiency vs. responsiveness trade-off.

3 · Motor & ESC Selection

Matching brushless DC motors to the propeller load curve using KV ratings, torque constants, and efficiency maps. ESC selection based on current draw at hover and full-throttle burst.

4 · Control Architecture

Cascaded PID design: outer position/altitude loop, inner attitude loop. Linearisation around hover enabling frequency-domain analysis and gain tuning.

5 · Battery & Power Budget

LiPo cell selection based on C-rating, capacity, and target flight time. Full power breakdown across motors, FC, and peripherals.

Tools

  • Python (NumPy, SciPy, Matplotlib)
  • MATLAB / Simulink
  • Pen & paper — still the best simulation tool

Outcome

A fully documented design specification (frame size, motor KV, propeller geometry, battery config, estimated hover time) that fed directly into the practical build.
