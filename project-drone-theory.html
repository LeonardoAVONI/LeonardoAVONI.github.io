Personal Project · 2023–2024

First Drone Development
Theory

From Newton's laws to motor equations — building the mathematical foundations of a quadcopter from scratch.

Overview

Before touching a single component, I committed to fully understanding the physics behind a quadcopter: rigid-body dynamics, rotor aerodynamics, motor-propeller matching, and linear control design.

1 · Rigid-Body Dynamics

6-DOF Newton-Euler equations of motion. Translational and rotational dynamics coupled through the inertia tensor and gyroscopic effects of spinning rotors.

2 · Rotor Aerodynamics

Blade Element Momentum Theory (BEMT) to size propellers for a target thrust-to-weight ratio: blade pitch, chord distribution, tip speed, efficiency vs. responsiveness.

3 · Motor & ESC Selection

Matching BLDC motors to propeller load curves via KV ratings, torque constants, and efficiency maps. ESC sizing based on hover and full-throttle current draw.

4 · Control Architecture

Cascaded PID design: outer position/altitude loop, inner attitude loop. Linearisation around hover for frequency-domain analysis.

5 · Power Budget

LiPo cell selection by C-rating, capacity, and target flight time. Full consumption breakdown across motors, FC, and peripherals.

Tools

  • Python (NumPy, SciPy, Matplotlib)
  • MATLAB / Simulink

Outcome

A complete design specification feeding directly into the practical build.
