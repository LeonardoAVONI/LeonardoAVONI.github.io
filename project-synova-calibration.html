Industry Internship · Synova SA, Geneva · 2021

Synova SA Internship
Laser Jet Calibration

Precision calibration of laser microjet cutting systems at Synova SA — where optics meets fluid mechanics.

About Synova

Synova SA is a Swiss company pioneering Laser MicroJet (LMJ) technology — a unique process that guides a laser beam inside a hair-thin water jet, combining the precision of laser cutting with the cooling and debris-removal benefits of water. Applications include aerospace turbine blades, semiconductor wafers, and medical devices.

Calibration Project

My internship focused on the calibration and alignment procedures for the optical and fluidic sub-systems of Synova's machines. Precise co-alignment of the laser beam with the water-jet channel is critical: even micrometre-level misalignment degrades cut quality dramatically.

Optical Alignment

Development and validation of a step-by-step calibration protocol for beam-steering mirrors, focusing optics, and the coupling chamber where the laser enters the water jet. Measurement of beam quality parameters (M², waist diameter, divergence) using a beam profiler camera.

Fluidic Sub-System

Calibration of the water-jet nozzle pressure–flow-rate relationship. Measurement of nozzle orifice geometry (50–150 μm diameter) using optical microscopy to detect wear and ensure replacement thresholds are met.

Metrology & Documentation

All procedures documented in a calibration manual used by the service team. Statistical process control (SPC) charts established to monitor machine-to-machine variability.

Skills Gained
