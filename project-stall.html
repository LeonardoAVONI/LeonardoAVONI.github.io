Stall Implementation
for Flexible Wings

Bridging the gap between linear potential-flow models and the nonlinear stall regime for high-aspect-ratio aircraft.

The Problem

Tools like ASWING rely on linear aerodynamic models (lifting-line / vortex-lattice) that are fundamentally unable to predict stall — the aerodynamic breakdown that occurs beyond the critical angle of attack. For high-altitude, long-endurance (HALE) aircraft operating at low airspeeds and high lift coefficients, stall prediction is safety-critical.

Approach

Sectional Stall Modelling

Extension of the sectional aerodynamic coefficients (Cl, Cd, Cm) beyond the linear range using empirical nonlinear polars derived from XFOIL computations and literature data. Implementation of a smooth stall ramp to avoid numerical discontinuities in time-domain simulations.

Coupling with Structural Deformation

For a flexible wing, local angle of attack varies along the span as the wing bends and twists. The stall model must therefore operate section-by-section on the deformed geometry — introducing coupling between structural deformation and aerodynamic nonlinearity.

Dynamic Stall Effects

Investigation of dynamic stall (Theodorsen / Leishman-Beddoes models) for flexible wings undergoing gust-induced oscillations. This is particularly relevant for gust-load alleviation control design.

Status

This work is currently ongoing as part of my PhD research. Preliminary results show that stall significantly alters the dynamic behaviour of flexible aircraft at high lift coefficients, with implications for flight envelope protection.

Tools

ASWING · XFOIL · Python · NumPy · Matplotlib · MATLAB
