PhD Contribution · ISAE-Supaero · Ongoing

Stall Implementation
for Flexible Wings

Bridging the gap between linear potential-flow models and the nonlinear stall regime for high-aspect-ratio aircraft.

The Problem

Tools like ASWING rely on linear aerodynamic models that cannot predict stall — the aerodynamic breakdown beyond the critical angle of attack. For HALE aircraft operating at low airspeeds and high lift coefficients, stall prediction is safety-critical.

Approach

Sectional Stall Modelling

Extension of sectional aerodynamic coefficients (Cl, Cd, Cm) beyond the linear range using empirical nonlinear polars from XFOIL and literature. A smooth stall ramp avoids numerical discontinuities in time-domain simulations.

Coupling with Structural Deformation

For a flexible wing, local angle of attack varies as the wing bends and twists. The stall model must operate section-by-section on the deformed geometry — creating coupling between structural and aerodynamic nonlinearity.

Dynamic Stall Effects

Investigation of Theodorsen / Leishman-Beddoes dynamic stall models for flexible wings undergoing gust-induced oscillations — relevant for gust-load alleviation control design.

Current Status

This work is ongoing as part of my PhD. Preliminary results show that stall significantly alters the dynamic behaviour of flexible aircraft at high lift coefficients, with direct implications for flight envelope protection.
