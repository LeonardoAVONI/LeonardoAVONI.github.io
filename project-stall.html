Stall Implementation
for Flexible Wings

Bridging the gap between linear potential-flow models and the nonlinear stall regime for high-aspect-ratio aircraft.

The Problem

ASWING uses linear aerodynamic models that cannot predict stall. For HALE aircraft at low airspeeds and high lift coefficients, stall prediction is safety-critical.

Sectional Stall Modelling

Extension of Cl, Cd, Cm beyond the linear range using XFOIL polars and literature data. Smooth stall ramp avoids numerical discontinuities in time-domain runs.

Coupling with Structural Deformation

For a flexible wing, local angle of attack varies as the wing bends and twists. The stall model operates section-by-section on the deformed geometry.

Dynamic Stall

Investigation of Theodorsen / Leishman-Beddoes models for gust-induced oscillations — relevant to gust-load alleviation control.

Status

Ongoing. Preliminary results show stall significantly alters flexible-aircraft dynamics at high CL, with direct implications for flight-envelope protection.
