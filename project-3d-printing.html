Personal Projects · 2020–Present

3D Printed Projects
Design & Fabrication

A growing collection of functional and creative designs — from drone parts to artistic pieces — made possible by additive manufacturing.

Overview

3D printing is a core tool in my engineering workflow — for prototypes, final-use parts, jigs, and the occasional creative project. Below is a selection of notable work.

Engineering Applications

Drone Frame Components

Motor mounts, landing gear, camera gimbal frames, and antenna holders for the quadcopter build. Design criteria: vibration resistance, minimum weight, and impact toughness. Printed in PETG.

Wind Tunnel Fixtures

Mounting brackets and probe holders for the EPFL wind tunnel project — dimensionally accurate to ±0.2 mm and aerodynamically unobtrusive in the flow field.

Lab Tools & Jigs

Cable management clips, sensor holders, alignment jigs, and custom electronics enclosures. When off-the-shelf doesn't exist, printing it takes 20 minutes of CAD and a few hours of print time.

Creative Prints

Beyond engineering: geometric sculptures, personalised gifts, guitar accessories. Resin printing used for high-detail decorative pieces.

Key Lesson

The best designs are not the ones that look good in CAD — they're the ones that account for print orientation, support removal, layer anisotropy, and post-processing from the first sketch. Iteration is fast and cheap; use it.
