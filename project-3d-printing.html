Personal Projects · 2020–Present

3D Printed Projects
Design & Fabrication

A growing collection of functional and creative designs — from drone parts to artistic pieces — made possible by additive manufacturing.

Overview

3D printing has become an essential tool in my engineering workflow — not just for prototyping, but for final-use parts, jigs, and the occasional creative project. Below is a selection of notable prints.

Engineering Applications

Drone Frame Components

Custom motor mounts, landing gear, camera gimbal frames, and antenna holders designed in FreeCAD and printed in PETG for the quadcopter build. Key design considerations: vibration resistance, weight, and impact toughness.

Wind Tunnel Fixtures

Mounting brackets and probe holders for the EPFL wind tunnel project — parts that had to be dimensionally accurate to ±0.2 mm and aerodynamically unobtrusive in the flow field.

Lab Tools & Jigs

Cable management clips, sensor holders, alignment jigs, and custom enclosures for electronics projects. When off-the-shelf doesn't exist or is overpriced, printing it takes 20 minutes of CAD and a few hours of print time.

Creative & Artistic Prints

Beyond engineering, 3D printing opens doors to artistic expression. Projects include geometric sculptures, personalised gifts, and musical instrument accessories (guitar picks, capo modifications). Resin printing used for high-detail decorative pieces.

Process & Equipment

FDM printer (Ender 3 / Prusa-style): PLA, PETG, TPU · Layer height 0.1–0.3 mm
Resin printer: Standard and ABS-like resin for high-detail parts
CAD tools: FreeCAD, SolidWorks, Tinkercad (quick concepts)
Slicers: Cura, PrusaSlicer

Lessons Learned

The best designs are not the ones that look good in CAD — they're the ones that account for print orientation, support removal, layer anisotropy, and post-processing from the very first sketch. Iteration is fast and cheap; use it.
