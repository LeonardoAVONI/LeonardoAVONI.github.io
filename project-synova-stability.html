Industry Internship · Synova SA, Geneva · 2021

Synova SA Internship
Water-Jet Stability

Analytical and experimental study of water-jet stability — the fluid mechanics heart of Synova's laser microjet technology.

The Stability Problem

The LMJ process depends on the jet's coherence length — the distance before Rayleigh-Plateau instability breaks it into droplets. Maximising this is critical for deep-cut applications.

Rayleigh-Plateau Instability

Linear stability analysis: dispersion relation and growth rate as a function of wavenumber, jet velocity, surface tension, viscosity. Identification of the most-amplified wavelength.

Nozzle Geometry Effects

CFD (ANSYS Fluent) of internal nozzle flow to characterise exit velocity profiles and their effect on the initial disturbance spectrum.

Experimental Work

High-speed imaging (50,000 fps) at various pressures and diameters. Measured coherence lengths agreed well with the linear stability model.

Outcome

Nozzle geometry and pressure recommendations for maximum coherence per workpiece thickness — feeding directly into Synova's nozzle design guidelines.
