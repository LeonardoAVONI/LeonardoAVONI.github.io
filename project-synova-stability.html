Industry Internship · Synova SA, Geneva · 2021

Synova SA Internship
Water-Jet Stability

Analytical and experimental study of water-jet stability — the fluid mechanics heart of Synova's laser microjet technology.

The Stability Problem

The performance of Synova's Laser MicroJet process depends critically on the coherence length of the water jet — the distance over which the jet remains a stable, laminar cylinder before breaking up into droplets (Rayleigh-Plateau instability). Maximising this coherence length is essential for deep-cut applications.

Theoretical Framework

Rayleigh-Plateau Instability

Linear stability analysis of a cylindrical jet subject to surface-tension-driven perturbations. Derivation of the dispersion relation and growth rate as a function of wavenumber, jet velocity, and fluid properties (surface tension, viscosity, density). Identification of the most amplified wavelength and its dependence on nozzle geometry.

Influence of Nozzle Geometry

Study of how the nozzle exit velocity profile (plug flow vs. parabolic) affects the initial disturbance spectrum imposed on the jet. CFD simulations (ANSYS Fluent) of the nozzle internal flow to characterise the exit conditions.

Experimental Work

High-speed camera imaging (up to 50,000 fps) of jets at various pressures and nozzle diameters to measure coherence lengths experimentally. Comparison with theoretical predictions — good agreement confirmed the validity of the linear stability model for the operational parameter range.

Outcome & Impact

Recommendations for nozzle geometry and operating pressure to maximise jet coherence length for different workpiece thicknesses. Results fed into Synova's nozzle design guidelines.
