Academic Project · CERN, Geneva · 2017–2018

Mon TPE/TM en 15'!
CERN, Switzerland

A scientific communication project presenting cutting-edge physics research in 15 minutes — conducted in the shadow of the world's largest particle accelerator.

Context

The Travaux Pratiques Encadrés (TPE) is a French baccalauréat independent research project. Growing up in Ferney-Voltaire — literally metres from the CERN campus — made choosing a topic straightforward.

The Project

My TPE focused on the physics of particle acceleration and detection, drawing on CERN's public resources and site visits. The "en 15'!" format required distilling complex frontier physics into a clear, engaging 15-minute presentation accessible to a non-specialist jury.

Topics Covered

  • The Standard Model of particle physics (simplified)
  • Principles of electromagnetic particle acceleration
  • LHC ring geometry and operational parameters
  • Detector physics: how collision events are recorded
  • The discovery of the Higgs boson (2012) and its significance

Communication Challenge

Translating quantum field theory into intuitive analogies, building visual aids from scratch, and structuring a 15-minute narrative arc. Skills that proved surprisingly transferable to conference presentations years later — most recently at AIAA AVIATION FORUM 2025.

Outcome

Highly commended by the jury. The first time I experienced the satisfaction of making a complex technical topic genuinely understandable to a general audience.
