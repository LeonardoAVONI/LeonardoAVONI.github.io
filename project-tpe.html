Academic Project · CERN, Geneva · 2017–2018

Mon TPE/TM en 15'!
CERN, Switzerland

A scientific communication project presenting cutting-edge physics research in 15 minutes — conducted in the shadow of the world's largest particle accelerator.

Context

The Travaux Pratiques Encadrés (TPE) is a French baccalauréat research project requiring students to investigate a scientific topic independently and present their findings. Growing up in Ferney-Voltaire — literally metres from the CERN campus — made choosing a topic straightforward.

The Project

My TPE focused on the physics of particle acceleration and detection, drawing on CERN's public resources and visits to the facility. The challenge of the "Mon TPE en 15'!" format was to distil complex, frontier physics into a clear, engaging 15-minute presentation accessible to a non-specialist jury.

Topics Covered

The Standard Model of particle physics (simplified) · Principles of electromagnetic particle acceleration · The LHC ring geometry and operational parameters · Detector physics: how particle collisions are recorded and reconstructed · The discovery of the Higgs boson (2012) and its significance

Communication Challenge

Translating quantum field theory into intuitive analogies, building visual aids from scratch, and structuring a 15-minute narrative arc — skills that proved surprisingly transferable to conference presentations years later.

Outcome

Highly commended by the jury. More importantly, it was the first time I experienced the satisfaction of making a complex technical topic genuinely understandable — a skill I've continued to develop throughout my academic career, most recently at AIAA AVIATION FORUM 2025.
