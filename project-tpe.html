Academic Project · CERN, Geneva · 2017–2018

Mon TPE/TM en 15'!
CERN, Switzerland

Communicating frontier particle physics in 15 minutes — conducted in the shadow of the world's largest particle accelerator.

Context

The TPE (Travaux Pratiques Encadrés) is a French baccalauréat independent research project. Growing up in Ferney-Voltaire — metres from CERN — made the topic an easy choice.

The Project

Particle acceleration and detection physics, drawing on CERN resources and site visits. The challenge: distilling frontier physics into a 15-minute talk for a non-specialist jury.

Topics Covered

  • The Standard Model (simplified)
  • Principles of electromagnetic particle acceleration
  • LHC geometry and operational parameters
  • Detector physics: how collision events are recorded
  • The Higgs boson discovery (2012) and its significance

Outcome

Highly commended. My first experience of making a complex technical topic genuinely accessible to a general audience — a skill I've continued developing, most recently at AIAA AVIATION FORUM 2025.
